About the Project

Prepared

CENTAURI ASCENDAN

An interactive simulation environment (Roblox video game) based on the space habitat classes proposed by NASA (Class I: rigid pre-integrated, Class II: hybrid, Class III: inflatable or built in situ with lunar regolith). It includes a guide robot called "Luna-E", a virtual companion that explains complex concepts in a simple and accessible way, like a robotic astronaut accompanying the player on missions. In the future, it aims to evolve into an immersive virtual reality (VR) experience for greater realism and engagement.

Target Audience: General public, with emphasis on children and youth from Peru and Latin America. The project seeks to motivate those who feel inexperienced or distant from space exploration, fostering interest in cosmological, space, and innovative topics. Through intuitive mechanics, it inspires rediscovery of skills and aspiration for STEM careers, reducing the gap between fiction and space reality in underrepresented regions.

High-Level Summary

CENTAURI ASCENDAN is an interactive Roblox video game that simulates lunar and Martian habitats inspired by NASA projects, such as the Lunar Lantern from the Olympus program. It addresses real space challenges, such as cosmic radiation, extreme temperatures, and resource management, contrasting science fiction expectations with scientific realities. With the guide robot "Luna-E", it offers educational immersion and realistic physical effects, promoting STEM motivation for young people. It is essential for accessible education and future mission planning, with plans to expand to VR for a deeper experience.

Detailed Description

Problem It Solves

In Peru and Latin America, many children and youth lack interest in STEM careers due to the perception that space exploration is unattainable, distant, and exclusive to developed countries. This creates an educational gap, with low enrollment rates in sciences (according to UNESCO reports, only 30% of Latin American students choose STEM), aggravated by lack of accessible and motivating tools. CENTAURI ASCENDAN solves this by offering a free and interactive simulation that makes space science tangible, fostering curiosity and self-efficacy through fun and educational gameplay.

Storytelling: Inspiring Figures in Space Exploration

Aracely Quispe Neira: A Peruvian Star at NASA

Imagine a girl in a small town in Lambayeque, Peru, in the 1980s. Aracely Quispe Neira, born in 1982 in Marripón, grew up in a humble environment without advanced technological resources. However, at age 6, upon learning about the 1969 Apollo 11 mission (which landed on the Moon), she felt an irresistible call toward space. "From a very young age, I knew I wanted to dedicate myself to sciences," Aracely recalls in interviews. This inspiration, similar to how modern video games capture young people's imagination with fictional worlds like Starcraft II or space simulators, propelled her to overcome obstacles: high university costs, cultural beliefs that discouraged women in STEM, and migration to the U.S. With perseverance, she obtained scholarships thanks to her karate skills and studied Systems Engineering and Astronautics. Today, Aracely is a senior engineer at NASA, leading missions like the James Webb Telescope (launched in 2021), and the first Latina to command three space projects. Her personal brand, "Aracely Quispe Neira, a story that inspires," motivates thousands in Latin America. Just as Aracely was motivated by exploration stories, CENTAURI ASCENDAN uses video games to ignite that spark in Peruvian youth, demonstrating that space is not just for "experts," but for dreamers like her.

Nancy Grace Roman: The "Mother of Hubble"

Long before the Hubble Space Telescope revolutionized our view of the cosmos, another visionary fought to make it a reality. Nancy Grace Roman, born in 1925, was an American astronomer who was one of the first female executives at NASA. From a young age, she was captivated by the stars, but was often discouraged from pursuing astronomy because it was considered unsuitable for women. Undeterred, she earned her doctorate in astronomy and later became the first Chief of Astronomy in the Office of Space Science at NASA, and the first woman to hold an executive position at the agency. It was here that she laid the groundwork for space-based astronomy. When the idea of a large space telescope was met with skepticism, Dr. Roman used her expertise and political savvy to champion the project, organizing astronomers and engineers, convincing Congress of its value, and shepherding it through its early development stages. Her tireless advocacy was instrumental in the creation of the Hubble Space Telescope, which has since provided breathtaking images and groundbreaking discoveries for decades. Nancy Grace Roman's story is a testament to breaking barriers and having the vision to see beyond the horizon, inspiring a new generation to look up and dream big.

General Objective

To foster interest and education in sustainable space exploration among children and youth from Peru and Latin America, through an interactive simulation that integrates scientific concepts from NASA and promotes STEM vocations.

Specific Objectives

  • Educate about space habitat classes (I-III) and physical/chemical variables (e.g., radiation, airtightness, artificial atmosphere with ECLSS, water management via Sabatier and hydroponic foods), allowing real-time visualization of effects.
  • Integrate the robot "Luna-E" as an educational guide to explain real challenges, such as radiation protection with polyethylene and water, or temperatures from -270°C to +120°C.
  • Motivate underrepresented users through gamified mechanics, with sustainability metrics and rewards, preparing for a future VR version.
  • Contribute to educational equity, measuring impact through metrics such as playtime and user feedback.

Contributions to SDGs (Sustainable Development Goals)

The project aligns with several UN SDGs:

  • SDG 4: Quality Education – Provides inclusive and accessible learning about space sciences, free on Roblox, reaching children without formal resources.
  • SDG 5: Gender Equality – Inspires girls and women in STEM, highlighting figures like Aracely Quispe and Nancy Roman, and promoting inclusive ergonomic designs in microgravity.
  • SDG 9: Industry, Innovation and Infrastructure – Fosters innovation in sustainable habitats, using NASA data to simulate autonomous construction (MMPACT) and in-situ resources.
  • SDG 10: Reduced Inequalities – Reduces regional gaps by making space exploration accessible to youth from Peru/Latin America, aligned with CSA educational toolkits.

What It Does and How It Works

The game allows designing and simulating 3D habitats in lunar/Martian environments. Guided by "Luna-E", users build structures with Roblox tools, apply variables (cosmic radiation that damages health/equipment, airtightness to maintain atmosphere, microgravity affecting mobility) and observe realistic effects, such as temporal degradation or inflatable expansion. It includes simulation of net habitable volume, crew, and chemical resources (e.g., 21% oxygen/78% nitrogen recycled with ECLSS). The interface is intuitive, with tutorials for children, and VR plans integrate sensors for sensory immersion.

Meet the NPCs: WALL-E

To make learning about sustainability even more engaging, we've introduced a special Non-Player Character (NPC): WALL-E! Based on the beloved character from Disney/Pixar, WALL-E appears in the game's resource management modules. He helps players understand the critical importance of recycling and waste management in a closed-loop environment like a space habitat. Players can assist WALL-E in collecting and processing waste materials, which are then converted into useful resources for habitat maintenance and expansion through simulated systems like the Sabatier process. This fun, interactive element teaches valuable lessons about sustainability and resource efficiency, making complex concepts accessible and memorable for players of all ages.

Detailed Use of NASA Resources

In the "CENTAURI ASCENDAN" project, NASA resources are strategically and multifacetedly integrated to ensure that the Roblox simulation is not only educational but also scientifically accurate and aligned with real space exploration standards. These resources, from public documents, 3D models, and technical guides, are used to model space habitats (Classes I-III) and simulate physical and chemical variables.

3D Models and Initial Visualizations

They are imported directly into Blender to create habitat prototypes (e.g., the ovoid Lunar Lantern with hexagonal shell printed in lunar regolith). In Roblox, these become interactive objects for users to build and modify structures, observing effects such as inflatable expansion (Class III).

Technical Documents for Variable Simulation

These guide simulation mechanics. For instance, habitable volume is calculated in real-time to evaluate if a habitat supports X crew members, integrating variables like radiation (shielding with polyethylene/water to reduce cancer risks), extreme temperatures (-270°C to +120°C with thermal control), airtightness (leak-proof materials), and microgravity (ergonomic designs for mobility).

Integration into Game Mechanics

Data is translated into Lua scripts in Roblox for dynamic effects: e.g., cosmic radiation "damages" the habitat over time, reducing sustainability metrics. The evolution of designs (1997-present) is reflected in a "historical" mode where users see progression from rigid to inflatable.

Importance of AI in the Project

AI plays a pivotal role in "CENTAURI ASCENDAN", accelerating development, improving accessibility, and enhancing creativity while maintaining an ethical focus. Its importance lies in three pillars:

1. Development and Content Efficiency

Tools like Chat Qwen generated Lua script drafts to simulate variables and educational writing for "Luna-E". OpenArt AI created hyperrealistic textures for Class II habitats. Grok synthesized NASA/CSA information, translating complex documents into simple explanations for in-game tutorials.

2. Educational and Inclusive Improvement

"Luna-E" incorporates AI for dynamic responses: adapts explanations to user level (e.g., for children, simplifies ECLSS as "magic air recycler"). In the future VR version, AI could generate personalized narratives, such as missions based on player interests.

3. Innovation and Scalability

Facilitates VR expansion, using AI for procedural generation (e.g., variable lunar environments). Contributes to SDG 9 (innovation), aligning with NASA's use of AI in real missions (e.g., JWST data analysis).

Critical and Integral Criteria for Resource and AI Use

To ensure integrity, rigorous ethical and technical criteria are applied, based on NASA guidelines and AI standards:

1. Scientific Accuracy and Verification (Critical)

All NASA data is directly cited and validated with primary sources; simulations are tested against real equations. Criterion: >95% fidelity, verified by expert review.

2. Transparency and Attribution (Integral)

NASA resources are attributed in game credits and forms. AI content is explicitly marked (e.g., "Texture generated by OpenArt AI") to avoid misinformation.

3. Accessibility and Inclusivity (Critical)

Content in Spanish/English, with "Luna-E" adapting complexity; minimum hardware (8 GB RAM) for regional equity. Criterion: User testing with Peruvians for usability.

4. Ethics and Sustainability (Integral)

NASA data is not modified; AI avoids biases. Complies with SDGs (e.g., no sensitive data exploitation). Criterion: Annual audit for positive impact, measuring metrics like young user retention.

5. Security and Privacy

Anonymous Roblox APIs; no personal data collection. Criterion: GDPR/Peruvian laws compliance.
